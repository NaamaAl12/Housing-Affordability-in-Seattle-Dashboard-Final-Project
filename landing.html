Mapping & Dashboard Tool

An interactive choropleth map and dashboard that visualize housing affordability trends across Seattle from 2006 to 2022. This tool helps users explore changes in rent, income, and affordability across neighborhoods to better understand housing challenges in the city.

View Dashboard

About Our Project

Seattle is a rapidly growing city known for its strong technology sector and diverse population. However, this growth has also created significant housing affordability challenges. Rising rents and limited housing supply have made it increasingly difficult for many residents to find affordable housing.

Housing affordability plays an important role in community well-being and economic stability. Changes in housing costs can affect where people live, neighborhood demographics, and access to opportunities. In recent years, Seattle has experienced major shifts in housing affordability driven by population growth, economic expansion, and housing policy decisions.

Our Features

Dashboard

The dashboard provides interactive visualizations of key housing indicators such as median rent, income levels, and affordability metrics.

Mapping

The project includes a choropleth map that displays different levels of housing affordability using color gradients. This allows users to quickly identify neighborhoods with higher or lower affordability.

Timeline Scale

A timeline slider allows users to select different years between 2006 and 2022 and observe how housing affordability has changed over time.

Filter by Demographics

Users can filter the data by demographic characteristics to explore how housing affordability differs across communities and population groups.

Bar Charts

Bar charts provide comparisons of housing affordability indicators across neighborhoods. These charts update dynamically based on selected filters and attributes.

Hover Popup

Interactive hover popups display detailed information for each neighborhood, including metrics such as median rent, income, and affordability indicators.

How The Map Works?

Explore the Map

Navigate the map by panning and zooming to explore different Seattle neighborhoods.

Pick a Year

Select a year using the timeline slider to view housing affordability for that time period.

Filter by Group

Apply demographic filters to examine affordability trends across different population groups.

Analyze Data

Use the charts and map together to compare neighborhoods and analyze affordability patterns.

Data Sources

The housing affordability data used in this project comes from publicly available datasets related to Seattle housing, income, and demographic trends. These sources help provide insight into how affordability has changed across neighborhoods over time.

Housing Data

Median rent and housing cost data from Seattle housing reports and regional housing datasets.

Income Data

Household income data from census and public demographic datasets used to calculate affordability indicators.

Geographic Data

Seattle neighborhood boundary shapefiles used to create the choropleth map visualizations.
